Modeling Earth Though a Lunar Month

What does the Earth look like from the moon? In this simulation (Schwieterman et al., in prep), we use the VPL 3D Spectral Earth Model to calculate disk-integrated views of the Earth from the moon as a function of time and wavelength. In a single month (actually 28 days), the moon orbits the Earth once and, therefore, we see the moon pass through all of the phases. But from the moon, Earth too goes through all phases. As a result, our 28 day simulation contains the full phase evolution of the Earth (large peaking trend in the right panel), as well as the daily modulation caused by oceans and contenients rotating in-and-out of view (periodic bumps and wiggles in the right panel). The rich spectral and temporal complexity of the Earth captured in this simulation allows us to study the Earth as if it were an extrasolar planet orbiting a nearby star. By studying the Earth we sharpen the techniques used to recognize similar, yet alien worlds.
